# Holyoke Veterans — from Wyatt E. Harper, *The Story of Holyoke*

**Source:** Wyatt E. Harper, *The Story of Holyoke* (written 1948; 1973 Centennial edition).
Internet Archive: https://archive.org/details/storyofholyoke00harp
**Purpose:** Master list of Holyoke veterans named in the book, for cross-referencing the memorial map project.

**STATUS:** Complete extraction — all wars the book covers (Revolutionary → Vietnam).
**Note on names:** Revolutionary/Civil/Spanish-American/WWI names are cleanly printed. The **WWII roster is a two-column scan and the OCR garbled ~a dozen names/dates** — those are flagged `[verify]`. Never assert a garbled name/date as fact; read it off the page image first.

## SPANISH-AMERICAN WAR (1898) — died in service

Holyoke's Company D, 2nd Massachusetts Regiment (Capt. William J. Crosier), mustered in May 3, 1898; fought at San Juan and El Caney. Those who died before El Caney or in other skirmishes:

Sergt. Edgar R. Train · Sergt. George A. Collier · Sergt. Frank G. Mattice · Sergt. Samuel C. Bogart · Corp. Ruel H. Coit · Allen B. Chamberlain · Joseph Bonneville · Timothy J. Canavan · Anatole Dugas · Samuel P. Hazelwood · Richard Mackey

## WORLD WAR II — casualty roster

Aggregate facts (stated by Harper): **~7,800 Holyokers served. 211 total casualties.** Four were killed at Pearl Harbor, Dec 7, 1941. Chaplain **Ignatius Maternowski** was the only Holyoke chaplain to give his life in WWII (killed shortly after Normandy).
**Congressional Medal of Honor winners:** Raymond O. Beaudoin · Joseph E. Muller.

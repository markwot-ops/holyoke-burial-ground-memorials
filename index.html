Holyoke Veterans — Burial Ground Memorial Maps

Documenting those who served, one stone at a time

These interactive maps document veterans buried in Holyoke's historic burial grounds. Each entry is drawn from the gravestone itself — name, rank, unit, and era — amplified by historical research. Maps are added as field work is completed.

Calvary Cemetery
1040 Northampton Street  ·  Est. c. 1870s
Holyoke's principal Catholic burial ground, serving Sacred Heart, Our Lady of the Holy Rosary, and Blessed Sacrament parishes. Spans the Civil War through Vietnam.
375 veterans documented

Elmwood Cemetery
2190 Northampton Street  ·  Oldest burial 1755
The oldest burial ground in Holyoke, given to the city in 1955. Home to veterans of the French & Indian War, Revolutionary War, and Civil War through World War II — including two veterans killed in 1777 and a WWI Navy Yeomanette.
44 veterans documented

Forestdale Cemetery
304 Cabot Street  ·  Est. 1860
Holyoke's public secular cemetery, organized in 1860 and dedicated in 1862. Contains GAR plots, a potter's field, and the grave of Medal of Honor recipient John MacKenzie (WWI).
243 veterans documented

St. Jerome Cemetery
125 Saint Jerome Avenue  ·  Est. 1864
Opened in 1864, originally known as the Catholic Cemetery of Holyoke. Served Irish and French Canadian immigrant communities. Eight victims of the 1875 Precious Blood Church fire are buried here.
In Progress
Rock Valley Cemetery
Rock Valley Road  ·  Some burials from 1700s
A small burial ground in West Holyoke near the Southampton line, with early interments dating to the 18th century.

Awaiting Their Resting Place
Burial location unknown  ·  Research ongoing
Holyoke's own who gave their lives in war and whose graves have not yet been located, listed alphabetically — those approved for listing and awaiting geolocation, and those identified and of record for whom research is ongoing. Each is honored here until his resting place is found.
